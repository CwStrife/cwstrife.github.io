Brightwell calculators

Two of Brightwell's most-used dosing products. Each one cites the dated label it was transcribed from.

Alkalin8.3 Liquid alkalinity buffer · per official label dated 2025
View label →
Add — ml over 4–6 hours
How this works: Brightwell Alkalin8.3 raises alkalinity by 1.4 dKH per 1 mL added per gallon (3.785 L). For tanks under 100 gallons, dose into a high-flow area. For new tanks or tanks below 6 dKH, halve this dose and re-test in 12 hours.
Calcion-P (Powder) Concentrated calcium · per official label dated 2025
View label →
Add — g predissolved in RO/DI
How this works: Brightwell Calcion-P raises calcium by 18 ppm per 1 g added per gallon. Always predissolve in RO/DI water before adding. Never dose dry powder directly. If your alkalinity is below 7 dKH, raise alk first — calcium and alk consume each other.

Standard 2-part recipe. Soda Ash for alkalinity, Calcium Chloride for calcium, dosed daily based on tank consumption.

Soda Ash + Calcium Chloride (2-part) Standard BRS Pharma recipe

Part 2 (Cal) — ml/day
How this works: Standard BRS recipe maintains a 1:1 ratio between Part 1 (alk) and Part 2 (calcium) consumption. Each 1 mL of Part 1 raises alk by 1.05 dKH per gallon. Always dose Part 1 and Part 2 at opposite ends of the tank, never together. Split into 4–6 doses per day if possible — a doser is the easy answer.
